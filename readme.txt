I’m thrilled to share a project I’ve been working on recently – BioVerse AI, a smart medical literature aggregator. As someone passionate about making research more accessible, I wanted to create a tool that could help researchers, clinicians, and students stay on top of the latest medical advancements without spending hours digging through papers.

BioVerse AI is designed to automatically fetch, analyze, and summarize medical research using AI. It pulls data from PubMed, Semantic Scholar, OpenAlex, and ongoing clinical trials, and then generates a structured, easy-to-read PDF report.

Some highlights that I’m especially proud of:

🔹 Compiles verified research papers on any medical topic

🔹 Identifies new drugs, experimental therapies, and ongoing clinical trials

🔹 Analyzes trends, gaps, and future research directions

🔹 Produces a ready-to-download, comprehensive PDF report

Behind the scenes, the system uses LangGraph + LangChain for orchestrating AI agents, Groq LLM for summarization, and Streamlit for a user-friendly interface.

Working on this project was an amazing experience – it taught me a lot about AI workflows, biomedical research, and how technology can make knowledge more accessible. I hope BioVerse AI can save researchers time and help them focus on what truly matters: advancing healthcare.
